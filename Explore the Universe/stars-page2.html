Life Cycle of Stars

Stars have a life cycle that begins with a stellar nebula, progresses through main-sequence, red giant, and ends as a white dwarf, neutron star, or black hole, depending on the initial mass. Massive stars explode in supernovae, seeding the cosmos with heavy elements.

Stars undergo nuclear fusion, converting hydrogen into helium and releasing tremendous energy in the process. This fusion process is what makes stars shine brightly for billions of years.
