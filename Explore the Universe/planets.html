The Fascinating World of Planets

Planets are celestial bodies orbiting stars, held in place by gravity. They can be rocky, like Earth and Mars, or gaseous, like Jupiter and Saturn. Our solar system consists of eight planets, each with unique characteristics and atmospheres.

Planets are divided into two main types: terrestrial planets, which are rocky and have solid surfaces, and gas giants, which are composed mostly of hydrogen and helium. Dwarf planets, such as Pluto, add another layer of diversity to the planetary family.
