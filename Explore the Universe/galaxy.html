The Mysteries of Galaxies

Galaxies are immense systems of stars, planets, dust, and gas held together by gravity. They are the building blocks of the universe, containing billions of stars and planetary systems. Some of the most well-known galaxies are the Milky Way, Andromeda, and the Triangulum Galaxy.

There are three main types of galaxies: spiral, elliptical, and irregular. Spiral galaxies, like the Milky Way, have a central bulge surrounded by spiraling arms. Elliptical galaxies are more spherical or oval and have less structure. Irregular galaxies lack a defined shape and are often the result of galactic collisions or near misses.
