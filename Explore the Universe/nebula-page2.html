Nebulae Types and Formation

The process of star formation occurs within nebulae. Regions of higher density in the nebula begin to collapse under gravity, eventually forming a new star. These regions are known as star-forming regions or stellar nurseries.

The diversity of nebulae reveals different stages of stellar evolution. For example, supernova remnants like the Crab Nebula are formed from the explosive death of massive stars.
