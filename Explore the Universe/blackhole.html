The Enigma of Black Holes

Black holes are formed when massive stars collapse at the end of their lifecycle, resulting in an extremely dense region in space where gravity is so strong that not even light can escape.

There are different types of black holes: stellar black holes, supermassive black holes, and intermediate black holes. Supermassive black holes are found at the centers of galaxies and can be millions or billions of times more massive than our Sun.
